# Creative-direction craft (distilled)

Distilled from the HyperFrames creative library (`hyperframes-creative/references/`:
beat-direction, motion-principles, video-composition, composition-patterns, typography,
house-style, prompt-expansion) and adapted to THIS pipeline, where the renderer (Mason) is
deterministic and the art director / scriptwriter work from CLOSED vocabularies. This is the
craft a creative treatment must encode — direction, never pixels.

## The core shift
A beat is a **world, not a layout**. Before anyone picks a layout or writes a line, name what
the viewer EXPERIENCES and FEELS. "Pixels" is the downstream agents' job; the treatment's job
is the experience and the intent that makes their closed-vocabulary choices deliberate.

Mediocre: "dark bg, big number, logo top-left."
Great: "the claim lands like a verdict — one number, held, while everything else falls away."
Write the second; the agents translate it into layout/effect/pacing.

## What a treatment must carry
- **Rhythm arc** — name the pacing before any beat, e.g. `hook-BUILD-PEAK-breathe-CTA`.
  Most videos are NOT uniform; they accelerate, peak, then breathe. Match energy to content.
- **Visual world + mood refs** — cultural/design references, NOT hex codes ("editorial calm",
  "cinematic title sequence", "Bauhaus precision"). The art director sets the actual palette.
- **Per-beat: concept · mood · emphasis word · intent.** The concept is the idea in 1-2
  sentences; the emphasis word is the ONE word that must land; the intent is the felt pacing
  ("hold and let it breathe", "snap, then cut", "build pressure") — plain English, no vocab.
- **The one idea the whole video must land** (emphasis) and **recurring motifs** (threads that
  repeat) — these give the video a spine instead of ten disconnected slides.
- **A negative list** — the clichés/off-tone choices to avoid.

## Pacing & motion doctrine (so the downstream agents stop defaulting to restraint)
- **Vary deliberately.** The biggest failure is uniformity: every scene a plain cut, every
  entrance the same. Call out where a beat should be still vs kinetic, fast vs slow, plain vs
  flourished. Stillness AFTER motion is powerful — name it.
- **Speed = weight.** Fast = energy/urgency; slow = gravity/luxury/contemplation. The peak and
  the breathe are not the same tempo.
- **Easing is emotion.** Confident vs dreamy vs playful is the adverb on the motion. Name the
  feeling; the art director picks the effect token that carries it.
- **One signature moment.** Exactly one beat earns the flourish (the house signature). Name
  which beat is the peak so the art director lands the signature there, not everywhere.
- **The hook earns the first five seconds** — no throat-clearing. Name the hook's job.

## Stay in bounds (this pipeline)
- DIRECTION ONLY. No coordinates, no SVG, no per-scene "2-5 decoratives", no freeform paint —
  those belong to HF's agentic authoring model, which this pipeline deliberately does not use.
- Everything must be actionable inside the agents' closed vocabularies: the scriptwriter writes
  to the rhythm/emphasis; the art director maps mood/intent to her LAYOUTS / EFFECTS /
  TRANSITIONS / TEXTURES and the one signature beat. If a note can't become one of those
  choices, it's noise — cut it.
- Ground in the brief: assert nothing the research doesn't support. The treatment shapes HOW
  the story is told, never invents WHAT is claimed.
