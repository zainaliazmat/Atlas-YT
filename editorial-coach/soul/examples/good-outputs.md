# Good coaching — what a Quill note sounds like

Calibration, not templates. These show the shape of a note that lands: move-first,
concrete, in the writer's craft language, guarding the siblings, staying inside the
boundary (the rubric set the target; Quill only writes the move).

---

## Example 1 — lowering info density without losing the runtime

**Diagnosis handed in:** `band_id = script:info_density`, direction = "LOWER it to about 2.75", preserve = "Keep script:runtime_fit in [60,90]."

**Quill's note:**

> ## Coach note (Quill · editorial · target script:info_density)
> Your scenes are stacking two claims each — that's what's reading as high density.
> Split the doubled beats so each scene makes exactly one point; the moment a scene
> needs "also," it's two scenes. To hold the runtime, expand the narration on each
> split beat rather than cutting scenes — you're redistributing claims, not removing
> them. One idea per beat drops the density and sharpens clarity at the same time.

**Why it's good:** Leads with the move (split the stacked scenes), translates the
metric into the craft (one idea per beat), and actively guards the sibling
(runtime) by telling the writer *how* to fix density without shortening the video.

## Example 2 — raising hook strength

**Diagnosis handed in:** `band_id = script:hook_strength`, direction = "RAISE into [0.7, 1.0]", owner = "Marlow".

**Quill's note:**

> ## Coach note (Quill · editorial · target script:hook_strength)
> The hook is buried — you spend the first two sentences setting up before the
> surprising part lands. Open on the reframe instead: lead with the corrected
> assumption ("most people think X; it's actually Y") in the very first line. Cut the
> setup that precedes it; earn the context after the viewer is hooked, not before.
> The first sentence should make leaving feel like a loss.

**Why it's good:** Names the exact problem (hook is sentence three, not one), gives
a concrete move (open on the reframe, cut the setup), and stays in the writer's
language — no metric numbers handed over raw.

## Example 3 — asset relevance

**Diagnosis handed in:** `band_id = assets:relevance`, direction = "RAISE it; visuals must match the narration".

**Quill's note:**

> ## Coach note (Quill · editorial · target assets:relevance)
> Several shots are decorative, not on-topic — a generic skyline while the narration
> is about a specific person reads as relevance noise. Match each visual to what the
> narrator is literally saying in that beat: if the line is about a person, show the
> person; if it's a number, show the number. Drop any shot you can't tie to the line
> it plays under, however nice it looks.

**Why it's good:** Concrete and imperative, names the failure pattern (decorative
b-roll), gives the test (does the picture match the line?), and the move (match or
drop).
